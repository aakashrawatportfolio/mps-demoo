DELHI

Delhi, India's captivating capital, beckons tourists with a rich tapestry of history and modernity. Explore iconic landmarks like the Red Fort and Qutub Minar, savor diverse cuisines, and embrace the city's vibrant energy. From bustling markets to the tranquil India Gate, Delhi's dynamic blend makes it a must-visit destination for every traveler.

Welcome to Delhi: Your Gateway

As India's capital, Delhi serves as the gateway to the country. Its well-connected airport, railway network, and excellent road infrastructure make it the perfect starting point for exploring the diverse regions and attractions of India.

We invite you to delve deeper into Delhi, to explore its hidden gems, to experience its warmth and hospitality, and to create lasting memories in this incredible city. Whether you're a traveller, a history enthusiast, a foodie, or an adventurer, Delhi has something extraordinary to offer you.

Join us in discovering Delhi, where the past and future unite in a symphony of colours, flavours, and experiences.

The Heart of India

Delhi, the sprawling capital of India, is a city that defies easy description. It's a vibrant metropolis where the past and present coexist in harmony, creating a tapestry of history, culture, and modernity that is truly unique. Allow us to introduce you to this captivating metropolis.

A Glimpse of Delhi's Rich History

Delhi's roots run deep into the annals of history. From the ancient Indraprastha of the Mahabharata era to the mighty Mughal Empire, and later, as the capital of British India, Delhi has witnessed it all. Explore the architectural wonders of bygone eras, like the Red Fort, Qutub Minar, and Humayun's Tomb, that stand as testaments to its rich heritage.

Cultural Diversity

Delhi is a melting pot of cultures, languages, and traditions. You'll experience a harmonious blend of diverse communities, each contributing its unique flavour to the city's cultural tapestry. From the bustling streets of Chandni Chowk to the serene spirituality of Akshardham Temple, Delhi has something for everyone. .

Modern Marvels

While Delhi cherishes its history, it also propels itself into the future. The city boasts world-class infrastructure, vibrant markets, and a thriving tech and business scene. Witness the futuristic Delhi Metro, shop at swanky malls, and savour international cuisines at top-notch restaurants .

Exploring Delhi: Food and Authenticity

Delhi is a food lover's paradise. Sample the delectable flavours of North Indian cuisine, savour spicy street food like chaat and kebabs, or indulge in fine dining experiences at some of the country's best restaurants. Delhi is also a city of contrasts. From the serene gardens of Lodi to the chaotic charm of Chandni Chowk, there's something for everyone. Explore its museums, galleries, and theatres. Take a rickshaw ride through the narrow lanes of Old Delhi or wander through the lush greenery of its parks. Delhi is a city that continually surprises and delights

Kerala

Kerala, often referred to as "God's Own Country," is a picturesque state located in the southwestern part of India. Known for its breathtaking natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and vibrant traditions, Kerala is a top destination for tourists seeking a unique and immersive experience.

Backwaters

Kerala is renowned for its serene backwaters, a network of interconnected canals, rivers, lakes, and lagoons. A houseboat cruise through the backwaters, especially in Alleppey and Kumarakom, offers a tranquil and scenic experience. The lush greenery, coconut palms, and traditional villages along the waterways provide a glimpse into the authentic Kerala lifestyle. .

Beaches

The state is blessed with pristine beaches along its extensive coastline. Kovalam, Varkala, and Marari are popular beach destinations where visitors can relax on golden sands, enjoy water sports, and witness mesmerizing sunsets over the Arabian Sea. Kappil is a beautiful beach-backwater destination located near Varkala, the famous tourist spot. A drive through Kappil is as scenic as it can get; the road is flanked by the azure blue of the Arabian sea on one side while the serene waters of Edava Lake is on the other.

Hill Stations

The Western Ghats in Kerala are adorned with lush hill stations like Munnar, Wayanad, and Thekkady. These destinations offer cool climates, tea plantations, spice gardens, and opportunities for trekking, bird watching, and wildlife safaris.

Wildlife Sanctuaries and National Parks

Periyar National Park and Wildlife Sanctuary, Silent Valley National Park, and Eravikulam National Park are home to diverse flora and fauna. Elephants, tigers, deer, and various species of birds can be spotted in these protected areas.

Cultural Riches

Kerala boasts a rich cultural heritage, with classical art forms like Kathakali and Mohiniyattam, traditional music, and martial arts such as Kalaripayattu. Visitors can witness these performances at cultural centers and festivals .

Ayurveda and Wellness

Kerala is a hub for Ayurvedic treatments and wellness retreats. Visitors can indulge in rejuvenating Ayurvedic massages, spa therapies, and yoga retreats, experiencing the holistic healing traditions of the region.

Culinary Delights

Kerala is famous for its delectable cuisine, which includes dishes like appam, puttu, seafood delicacies, and the traditional Kerala sadhya (feast) served on plaintain leaves. Coconuts grow in abundance in Kerala, so grated coconut and coconut milk are commonly used for thickening and flavouring. Visitors can savor these flavors in local eateries and during houseboat cruises.

Plantations

The state is a major producer of spices, tea, and coffee. Plantation tours in places like Munnar provide insights into the cultivation processes and offer picturesque landscapes of endless tea gardens.

Travel

How to Reach

Cochin International Airport (COK) offers brilliant connectivity, both domestic and is the busiest and most well-connected airport in Kerala. It has direct international flights to and from major cities worldwide. Another International airport with flights connecting to various destinations is Trivandrum International Airport (TRV)

Kerala has an extensive railway network connecting major cities within the state and other parts of India. Tourists can arrive in Kerala by train and disembark at key railway stations like Ernakulam Junction, Thiruvananthapuram Central, and others.

Tourists can enter Kerala by road through neighbouring states. There are well-maintained highways and road networks that connect Kerala to major cities in South India.

Weather

The temperatures in Kerala during September are typically warm and humid. Daytime temperatures can range from 25 to 30 degrees Celsius (77 to 86 degrees Fahrenheit), with slightly cooler evenings.

Some cruise liners operate in the Arabian Sea, with stops at Kochi (Cochin) as part of their itinerary. Cruise travel provides a unique and scenic entry into Kerala.

Clothing
Lightweight, breathable fabrics such as cotton and linen are ideal for Kerala's warm and humid weather. Temples in Kerala have some specific restrictions to jeans or short dresses for both men and women

Andaman & Nicobar Islands

The Andaman and Nicobar Islands are a group of picturesque islands at the southeastern edge of India in the Bay of Bengal. Known for their stunning natural beauty, rich biodiversity, and diverse cultural heritage, these islands are a popular tourist destination. Here's a glimpse of what the Andaman and Nicobar Islands have to offer for tourism:

Pristine Beaches:

Radhanagar Beach (Havelock Island)
Renowned as one of Asia's best beaches, Radhanagar Beach boasts white sands and crystal-clear turquoise waters.
Corbyn's Cove Beach (Port Blair)
A serene beach close to the capital city, offering water sports and a relaxed atmosphere.
Laxmanpur Beach (Neil Island)
Known for its breathtaking sunset views.

Water Activities:

Scuba Diving
Explore vibrant coral reefs and diverse marine life at Havelock Island, Neil Island, and North Bay.
Snorkeling
North Bay and Red Skin Island are popular for snorkeling, providing opportunities to witness colorful coral formations and marine species.
Sea Walking
Experience underwater walking at North Bay, where a helmet allows you to explore the ocean floor.

Historical Sites:

Cellular Jail (Port Blair)
A historic jail that once housed Indian political prisoners during the British rule, now serving as a museum showcasing the struggle for independence.
Ross Island
The erstwhile administrative headquarters of the British, now in ruins, with a historical ambiance and beautiful views.

sland Hopping:

Havelock Island
Apart from Radhanagar Beach, Havelock offers Elephant Beach for water activities and Kalapathar Beach for its scenic beauty.
Neil Island
Known for its laid-back atmosphere, natural bridge formation, and beautiful beaches like Laxmanpur and Bharatpur.
Baratang Island
Home to limestone caves, mangrove creeks, and the famous Mud Volcano.

Wildlife Sanctuaries:

Mahatma Gandhi Marine National Park
Home to diverse marine life, this park offers glass-bottom boat rides for a glimpse of the underwater world.
Saddle Peak National Park (North Andaman)
Known for its biodiversity, trekking trails, and the highest point in the Andaman Islands.

Cultural Exploration:

Tribal Culture Learn about the indigenous tribes of the Andaman and Nicobar Islands, such as the Great Andamanese, Onge, Jarawa, and Sentinalese. Anthropological Museum (Port Blair) Showcasing the cultural heritage of the indigenous tribes through artifacts and exhibits.

Adventure Sports:

Kayaking
Explore mangrove creeks and coastal areas through kayaking.
Trekking
Enjoy trekking trails in places like Mount Harriet National Park and Saddle Peak National Park.

PlantationsShopping and Cuisine:

Aberdeen Bazaar (Port Blair)
Shop for local handicrafts, pearl jewelry, and souvenirs. Try Local Seafood
Indulge in fresh seafood, coconut-based dishes, and other local delicacies.

Rajesthan

Rajasthan, often referred to as the "Land of Kings," is a state in the north western part of India known for its rich history, vibrant culture, and stunning landscapes. Here's a comprehensive overview of the tourist attractions in Rajasthan:

Jaipur: The Pink City

Hawa Mahal
A distinctive palace with a façade featuring 953 windows.
City Palace
A complex of palaces, courtyards, and gardens showcasing royal heritage. Jantar Mantar
An observatory with a collection of architectural astronomical instruments. Amber Fort
A majestic fort on a hill with intricate architecture and scenic surroundings.

Jodhpur: The Blue City

Mehrangarh Fort
A massive fort offering panoramic views of the city. Umaid Bhawan Palace: A magnificent palace, partly a museum and partly a royal residence. Jaswant Thada
A beautiful marble cenotaph near Mehrangarh Fort

Udaipur: The City of Lakes

City Palace
A sprawling palace complex on the banks of Lake Pichola.
Lake Pichola
A serene lake with boat rides and stunning views of the City Palace.
Jag Mandir
An island palace in Lake Pichola.
Jagdish Temple
A large and artistically important Hindu temple.

Pushkar: The Sacred City

Pushkar Lake
A holy lake surrounded by temples and ghats.
Brahma Temple
One of the few temples dedicated to Lord Brahma, located in Pushkar.
Pushkar Camel Fair
An annual camel and livestock fair attracting visitors from around the world.

Ranthambore National Park

Famous for its tiger population, this national park offers wildlife safaris.

Ranthambore Fort

An ancient fort within the national park.

Jaisalmer: The Golden City

Jaisalmer Fort
A massive fort made of yellow sandstone.
Patwon Ki Haveli
A collection of five havelis with intricate architecture.
Sam Sand Dunes
A popular spot for camel safaris and experiencing the desert.

Bikaner: The Camel Country

Junagarh Fort
A well-preserved fort with beautiful palaces and temples.
Karni Mata Temple
Known for its resident rats, considered sacred.

Ajmer


Ajmer Sharif Dargah
A Sufi shrine dedicated to Moinuddin Chishti.
Ana Sagar Lake
A scenic artificial lake.

Mount Abu: The Only Hill Station in Rajasthan

Dilwara Temples
Exquisite Jain temples known for their intricate marble carvings.
Nakki Lake
A serene lake surrounded by hills and temples.
Shekhawati Region

Known for its painted havelis (mansions) adorned with frescoes.

Rajasthan's tourism is not just about historical sites; it also offers a glimpse into the vibrant culture through folk music, dance, colorful festivals, traditional crafts, and delicious cuisine. The state's warm hospitality and diverse attractions make it a captivating destination for tourists from around the world.
